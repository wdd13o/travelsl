Getting Around in Sierra Leone

  • 🚗 By Car
  • 🚌 By Bus
  • 🚤 By Boat
  • 🏍️ By Motorbike
  • 👣 By Foot

🚗 By Car

  • Renting a Car: Renting a car is a great way to explore Sierra Leone, especially for remote areas. You can rent cars in Freetown and major cities.
  • 4x4 Vehicles: Rent a 4x4 vehicle for off-road areas like the Loma Mountains or Kono District.
  • Driving: Be prepared for busy traffic in Freetown and rural roads that are scenic but quiet.
  • Driver's License: An international driver's permit is required to rent a car.

🚌 By Bus

  • Local Buses: Public buses are an affordable option for short distances, but they can be crowded and schedules aren't always strict.
  • Intercity Buses: Long-distance buses like Babu Travel and Best Way Transport connect major cities like Freetown, Bo, Kenema, and Makeni.
  • Minibuses: Minibuses are popular for shorter distances but can be uncomfortable.

🚤 By Boat

  • Ferry Services: Ferries connect coastal and island destinations like Banana Islands and Tokeh Beach.
  • Private Boat Hire: Hire a private boat for a flexible experience to islands like Sherbro and Tiwai Island.
  • Boat Tours: Enjoy eco-tourism boat excursions to the Great Scarcies and Moa Rivers.

🏍️ By Motorbike

  • Okadas (Motorbike Taxis): Fast and efficient, but be cautious and wear a helmet. They’re great for navigating busy traffic in cities.
  • Hiring a Local Guide: Hire a local motorbike guide for off-the-beaten-path exploration, especially in rural areas.

👣 By Foot

  • Walking: Explore towns and cities on foot to immerse yourself in local culture, especially around markets and historic sites.
  • Hiring a Local Guide: Hire a guide to explore rural areas or hidden gems for cultural insights and safety.

Enjoy your journey through Sierra Leone! 🚗🚌🚤🏍️👣
